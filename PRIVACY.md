# Quick Palette — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: July 22, 2026_

Quick Palette is a keyboard-first command palette for Chrome. It is designed so
that **all of your data stays on your device**.

## What the extension accesses

To provide its features, Quick Palette reads the following through Chrome's
extension APIs, entirely on your machine:

- **Open tabs** (titles and URLs) — to list and switch between tabs.
- **Browsing history** — to suggest previously visited pages when you search.
- **Bookmarks** — to suggest bookmarked pages when you search.
- **Clipboard (write only)** — only when you use the "Copy current URL" command.
- **Local extension storage** — to remember which results you pick, so ranking
  improves over time. This usage data never leaves your browser.

## What the extension does NOT do

- It does **not** collect, transmit, or share any personal data.
- It does **not** send your tabs, history, bookmarks, queries, or usage data to
  any server. The extension makes no network requests of its own.
- It does **not** use analytics, telemetry, cookies, or tracking of any kind.
- It does **not** sell or transfer data to third parties — there is nothing to
  sell or transfer.

Web searches launched from the palette are performed by your browser's default
search engine, exactly as if you had typed the query in the address bar.

## Data removal

All stored data (learned ranking preferences) lives in Chrome's local extension
storage. Uninstalling the extension deletes it. You can also clear it from
within the palette via the "Reset learned tab ranking" command.
